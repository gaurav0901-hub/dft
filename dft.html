Introduction to DFT

What is DFT?

Density Functional Theory (DFT) is a way to calculate material properties using electron density, not tracking every electron’s wavefunction. It’s like finding the best recipe by tasting, not measuring every ingredient.

How DFT Works (Simplified)

Start with a material’s atoms, guess the electron density, solve equations (Kohn-Sham), and refine until it fits. The “functional” (e.g., LDA) is the secret sauce.

Why DFT Rocks Condensed Matter

It predicts band gaps, lattice sizes, and more—like how graphene’s magic was uncovered. Tools like Quantum ESPRESSO do this today!
