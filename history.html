History of Condensed Matter Physics

Early Foundations (1800s–1920s)

It began with crystallography—think Bragg figuring out how X-rays bounce off crystals—and Drude’s idea of electrons as a gas in metals.

Quantum Revolution (1920s–1950s)

Quantum mechanics changed everything: Bloch gave us electron waves, and band theory explained conductors vs. insulators. The transistor (1947) was born!

Condensed Matter Takes Shape (1960s–Now)

Superconductivity got its theory (BCS, 1957), soft matter joined in, and DFT emerged (Hohenberg-Kohn, 1964) to calculate material properties. Nobel Prize for DFT in 1998 sealed its legacy.
